About Me

I am a third year PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at Emory University. I am co-advised by Dr. Avani Wildani and Dr. Emily Wall where I work at designing models for knowledge transfer (Transfer Learning [Machine Learning] | Bayesian Belief Update [Data Visualization]).

My work with Dr. Avani Wildani is in collaboration with Dr. Yang Liu and Dr. Jianzhao Bi which focuses on knowledge transfer problems using domain adaptation and transfer learning. I use Instance Transfer Learning to improve PM 2.5 predictions for sensor deficient (data poor) regions across the world. We analyze how real-world data varies in complexity and the measures affecting their distribution, which in-effect provides insights into how transfer learning algorithms can be made more generalizable.

On the visualization front, my work with Dr. Emily Wall looks into Bayesian belief update and persistence of the belief update process, as well as how the user biases affect this persistence.

I did my Master's degree at IIIT-Delhi and worked with Dr. Vinod Scaria @CSIR-IGIB during my Thesis.
